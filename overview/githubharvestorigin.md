## 🧊 1. The “Dormant Blog / Abandoned Project” Layer (Free or Near‑Free)
These are everywhere — but invisible unless you go hunting.

### **Where they exist**
- Personal blogs last updated in 2014–2020  
- Indie hacker projects that never launched  
- Micro‑SaaS with 1–5 users and no revenue  
- Abandoned GitHub repos with working code  
- Expired or expiring domains with full content still indexed  
- Side projects from devs who changed jobs or burned out  

### **Why they’re gettable for free**
Owners:
- Don’t want to maintain them  
- Don’t want to pay hosting  
- Don’t care about the asset anymore  
- Feel guilty about “killing” the project  
- Are happy to hand it to someone who will revive it  

### **Acquisition method**
You reach out with a simple message:
> “Hey — I noticed your project hasn’t been updated in a while. If you’re not planning to continue it, I’d be happy to take it over and keep it alive.”

People say yes more often than you’d expect.

## 🧊 2. The “Digital Archaeology” Method (Zero‑Cost)
This is the most effective path for free assets.

### **How to do it**
1. **Search GitHub for abandoned SaaS repos**  
   Filter by:
   - Stars: 20–300  
   - Last commit: 2–6 years ago  
   - License: MIT / Apache / permissive  

2. **Search for abandoned blogs via Google operators**  
   ```
   "last updated" 2016 "blog"
   "powered by wordpress" "no longer"
   "site:medium.com" "last post" 2018
   ```

3. **Look for expired domains with content still indexed**  
   Use:
   - ExpiredDomains.net  
   - Wayback Machine  
   - Ahrefs free tools  

4. **Find dead micro‑SaaS on Indie Hackers**  
   Filter by:
   - “Stopped working on this”  
   - “Shut down”  
   - “Paused”  

5. **Search Product Hunt for projects with no traction**  
   Anything launched 2015–2022 with <50 upvotes is often abandoned.

### **Why this works**
Most creators never think to sell. They simply walk away.
